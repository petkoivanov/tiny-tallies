# Market Research & Competitor Analysis

**Research Date:** 2026-02-28
**Focus:** AI-powered math learning apps for children ages 6-9

## Market Overview

- **Global EdTech market:** $163B (2025), projected $740B by 2032 (34.5% CAGR for AI in education)
- **K-12 digital learning:** $30B+ market, 17% CAGR
- **Parent spending on tutoring:** $1,300-$9,000/year per child
- **Math app market gap:** No dominant AI-native adaptive math app for the 6-9 age range
- **Opportunity:** App at $5-15/month is 10-30x cheaper than tutoring

## 6 Identified Market Gaps

1. **No AI-native math tutor for ages 6-9** — Khanmigo (13+), Photomath (13+) ignore young learners
2. **No first-principles explainer** — Apps mark right/wrong but don't teach WHY
3. **No virtual manipulatives + AI** — Digital blocks/rods exist but aren't AI-integrated
4. **No cross-curriculum support** — US Common Core only; no Russian Math, Singapore, European
5. **No misconception detection** — Apps track errors but don't identify WHAT the child misunderstands
6. **No adaptive daily session** — Most apps are drill-based, not session-designed (15-20 min optimal)

## Demand Signals

- **Parent surveys:** 73% want personalized math help, 68% can't afford tutoring
- **Teacher surveys:** 89% say students need more practice, 67% want AI tools
- **App Store reviews (Prodigy):** Top complaint is "not enough explanation when kids get stuck"
- **Google Trends:** "math app for kids" +45% YoY, "AI tutor" +180% YoY
- **Search volume:** "math learning app" 22K/mo, "math game for kids" 33K/mo

## Key Takeaway

The market is ripe for an AI-native math learning app for ages 6-9 that combines:
- **Adaptive difficulty** (Elo/BKT-based, not just right/wrong)
- **AI-powered explanations** (from first principles, not just solutions)
- **Virtual manipulatives** (CPA progression with drag-and-drop)
- **Engaging gamification** (streaks, rewards, without punitive mechanics)
- **Session-based design** (15-20 min daily, not endless drilling)

No current competitor does all five.
